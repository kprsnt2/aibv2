## The Sacred Strip: Understanding the Pitch

The 22 yards of the pitch, the hallowed ground where battles are fought, has played a pivotal role. Before the tournament, there was widespread speculation that pitches would be "roads," designed for high-scoring encounters, promising a feast of boundaries and sixes.

However, the reality has been quite different. The pitches have offered a proper, balanced contest between bat and ball, often favoring disciplined bowling and intelligent batting. This has made for compelling viewing, ensuring that every run is hard-earned and every wicket a triumph. Intriguingly, a trend has emerged: most matches have been won by the team batting first. This could be attributed to the lack of significant dew, which typically aids chasing teams in evening fixtures. This added layer of tactical complexity has further enriched the contests, making for a truly engaging World Cup.
